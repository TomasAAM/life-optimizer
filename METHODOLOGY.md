# Training methodology & evidence base

This plan trains a **hybrid endurance athlete** — peaking for **Hyrox (2026-08-02)** and a
parallel **half-marathon (21k)**, weighted equally. It leans on the deep hybrid/concurrent-training,
endurance, and strength literature rather than the thin Hyrox-specific research, and is individualized
to lab-measured lactate zones, periodized toward the race, and auto-regulated by recovery data.

Each claim below is graded for evidence quality. Where the strong evidence contradicts popular
folklore, the folklore is dropped.

## Principles & sources

### 1. Mostly-easy, polarized intensity distribution
Elites accumulate ~80% of training at low intensity, with a small but potent dose of hard work and
little "grey zone." This is why we cap hard days and keep easy days genuinely easy.
- **Strong** — Rosenblat et al. 2019, systematic review + meta-analysis of RCTs (polarized modestly
  > threshold). https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29863593/
- **Strong** — Casado et al. 2022, systematic review of elite distance-runner training (IJSPP):
  low-intensity dominant; performance predicted by easy volume + tempo/short intervals.
  https://journals.humankinetics.com/view/journals/ijspp/17/6/article-p820.xml
- *Caveat:* the polarized-vs-pyramidal edge is modest; the robust finding is the large easy fraction.

### 2. Threshold work to raise LT2
Sparing, high-quality threshold sessions anchored on the measured lactate threshold (LT2 ≈ 163 bpm /
4:34/km) raise the aerobic ceiling that serves both the 21k and Hyrox running.
- **Moderate / practice-derived** — the Norwegian lactate-guided / double-threshold approach
  (Bakken → Ingebrigtsen) is validated by results, not by RCTs. https://www.mariusbakken.com/the-norwegian-model.html
  Its components (threshold work + high easy volume) are well supported above; the specific
  "double-threshold" packaging is expert practice, not proven superior in controlled trials.

### 3. Heavy & explosive strength for running economy
Heavy or explosive low-rep strength improves running economy and performance with minimal added mass.
With full-gym access we program barbell and plyometric work, not just station circuits.
- **Strong** — Llanos-Lagos et al. 2024, meta-analysis, running economy (Sports Medicine).
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38165636/
- **Strong** — Blagrove, Howatson & Hayes 2018, systematic review (Sports Medicine): economy +2–8%.
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29249083/

### 4. Concurrent training is compatible — interference is narrow
Running and lifting together do **not** meaningfully blunt maximal strength or hypertrophy. The one
real interference is on **explosive power (~28%), mainly in the same session** — so we keep
explosive/plyometric work OFF hard-run days, but otherwise train both freely.
- **Strong** — Schumann et al. 2022, updated systematic review + meta-analysis, *Sports Medicine*
  52(3):601–612.
- **Strong** — Concurrent training & hypertrophy 2022, systematic review + meta-analysis.
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9474354/

### 5. Gradual load progression (not a rigid ratio)
Build chronic fitness gradually and avoid abrupt load spikes. We use this as a heuristic, not a law —
the popular ACWR "sweet spot" is statistically contested.
- **Contested** — Impellizzeri et al. 2020, conceptual critique of the ACWR (IJSPP).
  https://journals.humankinetics.com/view/journals/ijspp/15/6/article-p907.xml
- Origin (for context): Gabbett 2016, BJSM training–injury paradox.

### 6. Taper into the race
Cut volume ~40–60% over the final ~2 weeks while keeping intensity, to arrive fresh.
- **Strong** — Bosquet et al. 2007, meta-analysis (*Med Sci Sports Exerc* 39(8):1358–65).
  https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17762369/

### 7. Hyrox-specific demands (treat as directional)
Hyrox rewards aerobic + anaerobic power, local muscular endurance, max strength, and running economy
*under fatigue* — hence the compromised-running sims.
- **Emerging** — Acute physiological responses & determinants in Hyrox 2025 (Frontiers in Physiology).
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11994925/ — small literature; directional, not settled.

### 8. Train station work at competition load (specificity)
Station strength-endurance is prescribed at the athlete's Hyrox division standard (Men's Pro: sled
push 202 kg, sled pull 153 kg, farmers 2x32 kg, sandbag lunge 30 kg, wall ball 9 kg to 3.0 m) so the
neuromuscular and metabolic demand matches race day. Heavy barbell lifts are loaded by RPE and
progressed individually. Exact kilos are individualized and autoregulated by the athlete's feedback;
the evidence backs the *method* (race-specific loading + heavy/explosive lifts for economy), not a
universal number.
- Reference — official HYROX station weights by division: https://www.hycrew.com/hyrox/weights

## How the generator uses this
The weekly generator references these **principles** (never inventing citations) to produce a
per-session `why` (justification + the trade-off) and a week-level `methodology` note. The sources
above are curated and rendered as a static panel, so a citation can never be hallucinated.
